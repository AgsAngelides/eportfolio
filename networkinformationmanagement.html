Network and Information Security Management


Introduction to the module

The 'Network and Information Security Management' module aims to provide the students with the necessary knowledge and hands on experience within the network technology subject. Concepts such as vulnerability assessments, penetration testing, forensic analysis and breach management will be discussed. The opportunity to evaluate ones personal development will also be provided.

Upon the completion of this module, students are expected to be able to:

  • Identify and analyse information security risks and vulnerabilities whilst also determining the methods, tools and techniques for managing/solving it at the same time.
  • Design and appraise computer programs for managing and auditing security issues.
  • Research and collate data from various security alert and warning sources to systematically analyse security breaches and issues.
  • Discuss issues faced by Cyber professionals, such as: legal, social, ethical, professional, etc.

Introduction

Loaded With Stuff! (loadedwithstuff.co.uk) is an e-commerce site offering two payment options: outright purchases and ‘Easy Financing’ program. It targets customers looking for the newest technology from known brands. In this report, the authors will describe the system, identify the security and privacy compliance requirements which apply to it, and provide a prioritised list of potential vulnerabilities and recommended mitigations.

System Description

Loaded With Stuff! was built with the Softaculous automated deployment system and is based on Loaded Commerce Community Edition v6.6. It is located at the A2 Web Hosting data centre in Amsterdam on a shared hosting server with IP address 68.66.247.187. The loadedwithstuff.co.uk domain name is registered through eNom LLC.

The website collects and stores data, some of which is considered personal, to be able to process the orders, such as: email address, delivery and billing addresses, phone numbers.

The website does not handle or store any cardholder data (CHD) and the payment page has instead been outsourced to a third party.

The ‘Easy financing’ finance program appears to be outsourced to an external provider and, thus, it is out of scope of this design document.

Governing Regulations

The Loaded with Stuff! website belongs to a UK domain; however, the web server is being hosted in the Netherlands (Group 1, 2022). Thus, the governing bodies and the regulations from both the UK and Netherlands were shortlisted as those applicable to the Loaded With Stuff! website:

  • The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), enforced by the Netherland’s Dutch Data protection authority (van Dam & Janssen, 2021).
  • The Data Privacy Act (DPA), enforced by the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) (ico., 2022a).
  • Payment Card Industry (PCI) Data Security Standard (DSS). It is developed by the global PCI Security Standards Council, and enforced through contracts (PCI, 2014).
  • Privacy and Electronic Communications (PECR) enforced by the ICO in the UK (ico., 2022b).

Security Vulnerabilities & Mitigations

The theoretical security vulnerabilities relevant to the above list of identified regulations have been prepared using STRIDE methodology and can be viewed below, where each vulnerability has been assigned to a STRIDE (Microsoft, 2022) category (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege) and ordered by business priority. The recommendations have also been prepared for each identified security weakness.

The hosting provider for the site, A2, provides a number of common security mitigations to all customers, including DDoS protection, firewalls, brute force defense, and virus scanning (A2 Web Hosting, 2022). These mitigations are not included in the table below as they are assumed to already be implemented.

Business priority has been determined based on each vulnerability’s DREAD risk ranking (University of Essex Online, 2022):

  • DREAD 0 (no risk), 1 (low risk) - low business priority.
  • DREAD 2 (medium risk) - medium business priority.
  • DREAD 3 (high risk) - high business priority.

STRIDE category Business priority Vulnerability description Recommendation
Elevation of Privilege High GDPR, DPA. Weak default Softaculous admin password and username.

When installing a new site, Softacolous sets default username to ‘Admin’ and the password is generated with capital letters, numbers and special characters (AuthorityRush, 2018). Update default username.		 Update default password to include lower case letters.
Tampering, Denial of Service High PCI. Several open ports exposed on server (Lee, 2022). Close all not needed ports (Gelnaw, 2019).

Switch to secure protocol formats (example, FTP to be switched to SFTP).

Deploy the site behind a web application firewall (WAF).
Tampering High GDPR, PCI. A06:2021-Vulnerable and Outdated Components.

Site uses Loaded Commerce Community Edition 6.6, which does not appear to have been updated since 2018. Software is no longer being maintained, and Google (2022) considers the Loaded Commerce site to be “dangerous and deceptive”.		 Use an e-Commerce software product that is up to date and maintained by its vendor. Implement good patching processes (OWASP, 2022a).

Elevation of Privilege Medium GDPR, PCI. CVE-2020-26886: Local Privilege Escalation using softaculous/bin/soft.

Spoofing Medium GDPR,PCI. Password policy is too weak.

It allows sequential, repeated passwords and dictionary words.

User accounts are not locked out after more than 10 failed login attempts.

No multi factor authentication is enforced.

Site will be vulnerable to a Brute force attack.		 Enforce strong password policy (Davin, 2020).

Spoofing / Information Disclosure Low GDPR. Email Phishing.

The site does not use the domain to generate emails: the email ID is from a different domain.		 Use of the domain mail ID (example Support@loadedwithstuff.co.uk) instead of the current account that sends the registration information.

Train staff to understand the risk of internal phishing, pretexting attacks.

Implement spoofing protection mechanisms such as SPF or DMARC (Dmarcly, 2022).

Conclusion

E-commerce sites remain one of the most highly prized targets for cyber threats, with security incidents growing by 20% in 2021 (Webscale, 2021). In this paper, the authors have listed a number of potential security vulnerabilities faced by the Loaded With Stuff! Website. The paper further provides a prioritized list of mitigations which the operators of the site should consider in order to secure their site against cyber threats while maintaining compliance with their legal and regulatory obligations.

Scanning Methodology

The list of vulnerabilities identified in the design document is not considered all encompassing, thus, to ensure all critical web vulnerabilities have been addressed in this executive report, the OWASP Top 10 (2021) was employed as a framework for performing vulnerability scans. The OWASP recommendations target the most critical exposures and are well documented and used within the industry (Fredj et al, 2021).

By using the OWASP Top 10, a standard security baseline is developed. It ensures the presence of critical security configurations and minimises risk by providing required technical and administrative controls that are implemented as required due diligence, in a proactive approach (Jenkins,2022). The OWASP top 10 provides any business with value through following the processes illustrated in our methodology in Figure 1 (RedScan Teams, 2020).
